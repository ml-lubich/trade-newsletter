Every week I talk to trade business owners — HVAC techs, plumbers, electricians, roofers. I ask them the same question: **"What's the thing you hate doing most that you know you should be doing better?"**

The answers are remarkably consistent. And almost none of them are well-served by existing software.

Here are five SaaS ideas that I think are under-built and over-demanded. Each has a clear path to $1M+ ARR. Some could be much bigger.

## 1. AI-Powered Warranty & Callback Tracker

**The pain**: When a customer calls back about a job done 6 months ago, most trade businesses scramble. Was it under warranty? What parts were used? Who did the install? This information lives across text messages, invoices, and someone's memory.

**The product**: A system that automatically ingests job data from ServiceTitan/Housecall Pro/Jobber, tracks warranty periods by equipment and labor, and proactively alerts when warranties are expiring. When a callback comes in, the tech sees the full history on their phone in seconds.

**Why it works**:
- Callbacks cost trade businesses $500-$2,000 per incident in labor alone
- Warranty tracking reduces repeat visits by 30-40%
- Insurance companies would pay for data on warranty compliance
- Stickiness is massive — once your warranty data lives somewhere, you don't leave

**Path to $1M ARR**: 1,000 businesses at $99/month = $1.2M ARR. Totally achievable in a single metro area for one trade vertical.

## 2. Apprentice Training & Compliance Platform

**The pain**: The trades are facing a historic labor shortage. The average age of a licensed plumber is 55. Companies desperately need to hire and train apprentices, but the training process is a mess of paper logs, state-specific requirements, and manual hour tracking.

**The product**: A mobile-first platform where apprentices log hours, complete training modules (video-based, AI-graded), and automatically track progress toward licensure requirements by state. Employers get a dashboard showing compliance status across their whole team.

**Why it works**:
- Regulatory requirement creates built-in demand
- State licensing boards could be channel partners
- Training content can be generated and updated with AI
- Employers will pay to avoid compliance violations ($10K+ fines)

**Path to $1M ARR**: Partner with 3-5 state licensing boards. 2,000 apprentice seats at $49/month = $1.2M ARR.

## 3. Smart Parts Inventory & Procurement

**The pain**: Every trade contractor has a truck stocked with parts. Managing what's on the truck vs. what's in the warehouse vs. what needs to be ordered is a nightmare. Most companies use spreadsheets or just... remember. Stock-outs delay jobs, and over-ordering ties up cash.

**The product**: Computer vision-powered inventory management. Techs snap photos of their truck stock, AI identifies parts and counts. The system learns usage patterns by job type and season, predicts what needs to be reordered, and auto-generates POs to preferred suppliers.

**Why it works**:
- Parts and materials are 30-40% of job cost in most trades
- 15-20% of parts spend is waste (expired, duplicate, wrong-ordered)
- Integration with supply houses (Ferguson, Winsupply) creates a marketplace opportunity
- Transaction revenue on parts ordering adds a second revenue stream beyond SaaS

**Path to $1M ARR**: 500 businesses at $199/month = $1.2M ARR. Add 2-3% transaction fee on $50M in parts volume and you're looking at another $1-1.5M.

## 4. Reputation Management Autopilot

**The pain**: Every contractor knows reviews are everything. But consistently asking for reviews, responding to them, and managing your online reputation across Google, Yelp, Facebook, Nextdoor, and Angi is a full-time job that nobody has time for.

**The product**: An AI-powered review management system that automatically requests reviews post-job (via text, timed to the optimal window), drafts personalized responses to all reviews (positive and negative), monitors mentions across platforms, and generates monthly reputation reports.

**Why it works**:
- Direct, measurable ROI — more reviews = more calls = more revenue
- Companies that respond to all reviews get 35% more engagement
- AI makes personalized responses scalable (no more copy-paste "Thanks for the review!")
- Natural expansion from review management to broader marketing automation

**Path to $1M ARR**: This is a high-volume, lower-price play. 3,000 businesses at $29/month = $1M ARR. Could easily 10x with upsells to marketing services.

## 5. Subcontractor Network & Payment Platform

**The pain**: Many trade businesses regularly need subs — a plumber needs an electrician for a bathroom remodel, a GC needs a reliable HVAC crew. Finding, vetting, scheduling, and paying subs is all done through phone calls, Venmo, and handshake deals.

**The product**: A private network for verified, licensed subcontractors. General contractors post job needs, subs claim them, work is tracked, and payment flows through the platform with proper 1099 documentation. Think "Uber for trade subcontractors" but without the race-to-the-bottom pricing — this is for quality, vetted pros.

**Why it works**:
- Payment processing creates transaction revenue (2-3%)
- Solves a real trust problem — you can see a sub's license, insurance, and past work
- 1099 compliance automation is a major pain point at tax time
- Network effects — the more subs and GCs on the platform, the more valuable it becomes

**Path to $1M ARR**: 1,000 GCs processing $100K/year in sub payments through the platform at 2.5% = $2.5M in transaction revenue, plus $49/month SaaS subscriptions.

## The Common Thread

All five of these ideas share key traits:

- They solve problems that **cost real money today** (not nice-to-haves)
- They're **vertical-specific** enough to build defensible expertise
- They have **clear, measurable ROI** that sells itself
- AI is a **genuine unlock**, not a gimmick
- They can reach $1M ARR with **fewer than 3,000 customers**

The trades market is where SaaS was 10 years ago. The founders who go deep here will build the next generation of vertical SaaS giants.
